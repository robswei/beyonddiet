5 Ways to Lose Weight During Menopause

If you’re going through menopause (or even perimenopause) and you’re having trouble losing weight, you’re not alone! Between the extreme hormonal fluctuations to the natural changes in metabolism as we age, it’s normal to put on a bit of weight. Fortunately (with just a few simple adjustments) that weight doesn’t need to stick around.

We've helped women just like you lose weight and get their health back for over 10 years. So, just follow our 5 tips for losing weight during menopause and you'll look leaner, be bursting with energy, and get through "the change" feeling better than ever.

Step 1: Upgrade Your Nighttime Routine

Without sleep, your cortisol levels go up. Cortisol not only makes you feel stressed, but it causes bloating and forces the body to ... hold on to excess fat. During menopause, between the hormonal changes, hot flashes, and night sweats - getting your 8 hours takes a bit more work. Create a Nightly Routine for Better Sleep About an hour before bed, put your phone or computer away. The blue light interferes with melatonin production and keeps you awake. After the phone is stashed, do a simple, relaxing activity. Whether you read a book, take a bath, or set aside a few minutes to write down your thoughts for the day, your mind will start to unwind and your stress levels will go down. Same Time. Every Night. At least 8 hours before you need to get up, put yourself to bed. Even if you don’t fall asleep right away, the fact that you’re resting for a full 8 hours will help your general energy and fat burning potential. Step 2: Build Muscle with Vinyasa Yoga

Make That Muscle

As we age, muscle mass declines. Since muscle burns about 3 times as many calories ... as fat, we want all the muscle we can get! Now, we’re not saying you need to become a body builder, but weight training is more important during menopause.
Yoga IS Weight Training Yoga lets you use your own body weight to get strong, lean muscles. Plus, it’s easy on your joints, increases flexibility, and helps reduce stress. No matter your flexibility, vinyasa yoga is great. In fact, it’s one of the most gentle forms of exercise around, so it’s perfect for all fitness levels. Step 3: Avoid Unfermented Soy

Fermented vs. Unfermented

Soy products like natto, miso, and tempeh go through a long fermentation process to become ... super gut friendly and full of nutrients. But things like soy milk, tofu, and soy sauce (the soy products we actually eat on a daily basis) are unfermented. These foods are hard to digest and contain harmful isoflavones that you should avoid during menopause.
Isoflavones Block Estrogen When estrogen isn’t absorbed properly, your hormonal levels are disrupted, and your thyroid can’t function adequately. All together - that means weight gain. How to Avoid Soy. For a dairy alternative, switch to coconut or almond milk. For vegetarian protein, try beans, lentils, and nuts. instead of soy sauce, use coconut aminos. It’s gluten-free, vegan, lower in sodium, and contains 17 healthy amino acids. Plus, it tastes exactly like soy sauce! Step 4: Shop These Special Sections of your Store

Menopause Makes You Hungry

As your hormones fluctuate, your appetite naturally increases. Instead of trying to shut down ... your appetite or greatly restrict your calories to lose weight during menopause, it’s better to let yourself eat the right foods.
Eat as Much As You Want...
From the Perimeter At the perimeter of the grocery store, you’ll find produce, meat, dairy, and eggs. These foods fuel your body with healing, nutrient rich produce and hearty protein that will help you lose weight and lessen aggravating symptoms. Avoid Processed Food. What’s not in the perimeter? Processed Food. Things like bread, microwave meals, and even 100 calorie snack packs are full of toxic, chemical ingredients that raise your blood sugar, harm your liver, and make it even harder to lose weight. Step 5: Enjoy Coffee the Right Way

Hot Coffee = Hot Flash

Coffee can trigger a hot flash. Strangely, it’s not just the caffeine that’s triggering. It’s the heat. ... When you drink something warm (even a plain glass of hot water), that sudden heat can set off a flash. Instead of drinking a steaming hot cup of joe every morning, switch to iced coffee. Coffee in Moderation Too much caffeine interferes with sleep and increases blood pressure and cortisol levels - all things that get in the way of weight loss. Thankfully, one cup of coffee is perfectly fine. It gives you a bit of caffeine for energy, but not enough to trigger the side effects. With an 8-ounce iced coffee to start your day, you’ll avoid hot flashes and keep your weight loss on track.
